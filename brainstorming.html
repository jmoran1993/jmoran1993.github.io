Economy with avalanches¶

Model¶

The model has $Q$ firms that buy and sell goods to each other (they need other goods to produce their own), and sell some of these goods to households that provide firms with labor.

The production $\pi_i$ of firm $i$ is given by:

$$ \pi_i = z_i\min_{j}(J_{ij}Q_j^i) $$

Where $J_{ij}$ is the input-output matrix (how much $i$ needs from $j$ in order to produce its goods), $Q_{j}^i$ is the quantity of goods that $i$ effectively buys from $j$ and $z_i$ is firm $i$'s productivity. This production function implies that goods cannot be substituted.

Assuming market clearing and competitive prices (zero-profits) for firms and utility maximization by the representative household leads to two equations :

$$ \mathbf{M}P = V \\ \mathbf{M}^t \lambda = \xi $$

where $P$ is the price vector and $\lambda$ the output vector, and $$ \mathbf{M}_{ij}= z_i\delta_{ij}-\frac{1}{J_{ij}}. $$ These equations should hold in some stationary state (no dynamics here yet).

Parameters¶

We work with undirected graphs, so that $\frac{1}{J_{ij}}\neq 0 $ if $(i\leftrightarrow j)$ is in the production graph $\mathcal{G}$, and $\mathbf{M}=\mathbf{M}^t$.

Furthermore

$$ (i\leftrightarrow j)\in \mathcal{G} \implies \frac{1}{J_{ij}}=\left\{\begin{matrix} \frac{C}{Q} &\mbox{ with prob. } q \\ C\sigma &\mbox{ with prob. } 1-q \end{matrix}\right. $$

For $\mathcal{G}$, we take either an Erdös-Renyi graph with average connectivity $k$ (in which case the spectrum of $\mathbf{M}$ is in a band of width $\sigma Q$), or a Watts-Strogatz small-world graph of connectivity $k$ and rewiring probability $p$.

We start with a matrix $\mathbf{M}$ generated with the rule given previously, and productivities $z_i=(1,\ldots,1)$.

We first solve the equation $\mathbf{M}P=V$ and find how many $P_i$'s are negative. We interpret these to be bankrupted firms.

We 'stabilize' this by gradually increasing the productivity $z_i$ of firms with negative prices, until a state where $\forall i \quad P_i>0 $ is reached.

This has the effect of making the spectrum of $\mathbf{M}$ positive with the smallest e.v. very close to 0.

Perturbations and avalanches¶

We now start from a stabilized state and perturb it. We look at the size of "avalanches": the number of prices that become negative after a perturbation.

Productivity perturbations¶

Take a random firm $j$, and do $z_j^{p}=z_j^{s} \xi$ where $\xi$ is a random number (i.e. uniform in 0.5,1).

Graph rewiring avalanches¶

We can also consider a very simple perturbation: take a link $(i\leftrightarrow j)\in \mathcal{G}$ and pick $(k,l)$ such that $(k\leftrightarrow l)\notin \mathcal{G}$ and "swap" the link so that $$\frac{1}{J_{kl}}\leftarrow \frac{1}{J_{ij}}$$ $$\frac{1}{J_{ij}}\leftarrow 0$$
